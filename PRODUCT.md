# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Lab researchers operating a neaSNOM setup and related galvo hardware during live alignment, manual positioning, and scan preparation. They use the interface at a workstation near the instrument and need quick confidence that each movement command and readback is correct.

## Product Purpose
Provide a dependable desktop control surface for connecting to galvo hardware, jogging XY and Z motion, monitoring live position, and running raster scans without relying on ad hoc notebooks. Success means researchers can move confidently, read state immediately, and avoid ambiguity while working with real hardware.

## Brand Personality
Precise, dependable, lab-grade. The interface should feel calm and technically trustworthy rather than expressive or consumer-polished.

## Anti-references
Do not make this look like a consumer mobile control app, a glossy marketing dashboard, or a playful maker-tool UI. Avoid ornamental effects, decorative color, oversized gestures, or ambiguous control groupings that slow down instrument work.

## Design Principles
1. Keep control groupings explicit so users can understand hardware scope at a glance.
2. Put live state next to the action that affects it, especially for motion and readback.
3. Prefer familiarity and legibility over novelty; the UI should disappear into the task.
4. Use emphasis sparingly and only to support connection state, active actions, and hardware feedback.
5. Preserve a dense but calm workstation layout that supports repeated expert use.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Maintain strong contrast for all labels and readouts, keep state changes visible without relying on color alone, and avoid decorative motion. Any transitions should remain reduced-motion-safe and the interface should stay legible during long bench sessions on standard lab displays.
